Tuna Casserole

This is another favorite chaildhood recipe of mine. If you love a creamy casserole, and love tuna then this is a recipe for you. The noodles are always so pleasently tender and creamy, the cellery adds a nice little crunch with every bite, and the onions add a nice enjoyable flaver to the dish. This is also a nice quick recipe, only taking about 35 minutes to complete so it is a good dish for the nights you don't have a lot of time to cook.

Ingredients

  • 1 12 ounce package of egg noodles
  • 1/4 Cup chopped onion
  • 2-3 Sticks of celery
  • 2 Cups shredded chedder cheese
  • 1 Cup frozen green peas
  • 2 5 ounce cans of tuna, drained
  • 2 10.75 ounce cans of condensed cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/2 of a 4.5 ounce can of sliced mushrooms
  • 1 Cup crushed potato chips or cornflakes cereal
  • Some salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil then add the pasta and let cook for 8-10 minutes.

  2. While waiting for the pasta to cook, chop up your onions and cellery.

  3. Preheat the oven to 425*F.

  4. In a large bowl, thoroughly mix the noodles, onions, 1 cup of cheese, peas, tuna, mushrooms, and mushroom soup. Then transfer the contents to a 9x13 inch casserole dish and top it with the potato chip crumbs or cornflakes cereal and add the remaining 1 cup of cheese.

  5. Bake the casserole for about 15 to 20 minutes in the oven until the cheese is bubbly.

  6. Take out the casserole and let cool before serving.
